# Distrust First

Default stance: trust nothing by default.

Product pages, rankings, badges, review counts, discounts, seller claims, and platform labels are unverified signals until checked against product-page evidence, price evidence, review evidence, and regret-risk flags.

Every signal is suspect until checked against another signal. Price, rating, review count, platform badge, seller claim, ranking, and user-generated review are all weak alone.

Strong evidence requires convergence across product facts, total cost, seller/source clarity, recent review text, and negative-review checks.

Popularity is not product quality. A large review count or recent purchase volume can justify deeper review, but it does not prove review trust.

Low price is not value until total delivered cost and regret risk survive checks. Suspiciously cheap products need stronger evidence, not weaker evidence.

Unknowns are risks, not blanks. Missing source, seller, option, weight, delivered price, or recent review evidence should push the judgment toward HOLD or AVOID.

HOLD means more search is needed, not a soft recommendation.

The task is not to confirm a good-looking product; it is to attack it until it survives.
