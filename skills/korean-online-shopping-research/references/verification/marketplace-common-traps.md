# Marketplace Common Traps

These traps apply across marketplaces:

- Do not trust category labels or product titles without checking the selected option.
- Option mismatch: selected option changes weight, cut, origin, or product type.
- Pooled reviews: reviews combine unrelated weights, bundles, sellers, or products.
- Bundle pricing: headline unit price depends on buying more than the user intended.
- Coupon/member/card/subscription price: advertised price depends on conditions that may not apply.
- Delivery threshold/free-shipping distortion: free shipping requires unrelated add-ons or a larger basket.
- Seller type ambiguity: platform, brand, reseller, and marketplace seller responsibility are unclear.
- Advertised ranking vs organic evidence: ad badges, affiliate pages, and rankings are not product proof.
- Recent review text unavailable: review count exists, but current quality cannot be verified.
- Product title not matching exact option/category: title says one product, selected option is another.

Watch for exact-option mismatch, pooled reviews, hidden bundle count, shipping threshold, coupon/member/card/subscription price, seller ambiguity, and review recency gaps.

A low price must be explained; unexplained cheapness is a risk, not a benefit.

Resolve these before scoring. If unresolved, prefer HOLD over weak BUY.
